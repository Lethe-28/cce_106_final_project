From Research to Reality

3. Conducting Market Research

This step involves systematically gathering data. It's a mix of primary research (going to the source) and secondary research (reviewing existing data).

What: The systematic gathering of specific data about your target customers, industry landscape, and competitors.

Why: To replace assumptions with factual evidence and deeply understand the competitive environment you are entering.

How: Use a mix of primary research (surveys, customer interviews, focus groups) and secondary research (industry reports and public data).

4. Analyze & Verify Results

Data is just noise until you analyze it. A SWOT analysis helps verify your position.

What: Processing, interpreting, and verifying the collected market data to derive actionable insights.

Why: To confirm *what* the market truly needs and *how* your idea must be adjusted (or pivoted) to maximize its chances of success.

How: Perform a rigorous SWOT analysis, create buyer personas, and develop a final, market-verified product hypothesis.

How: Define key roles and responsibilities, recruit specialists, and establish a clear delegation and accountability framework.
